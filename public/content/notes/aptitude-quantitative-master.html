Divisibility by 7 — Step-by-Step on 1001

Why div-by-11 uses alternating sums: 10 ≡ −1 (mod 11). So 10² ≡ +1, 10³ ≡ −1, … alternating. For number abcde: value = a·10⁴ + b·10³ + c·10² + d·10 + e ≡ a − b + c − d + e (mod 11). That's your alternating digit sum.

Why div-by-13 adds instead of subtracts: 10 ≡ 10 (mod 13), but 4×10 = 40 ≡ 1 (mod 13). So multiplying by 4 and adding creates a congruence that telescopes correctly for 13.

A.2   Primes, Factors & Euler's Totient Core

Testing Primality — Efficient Method

Primality Test Rule

To test if N is prime: check divisibility by all primes up to √N. If none divide N → N is prime.
Efficiency shortcut: All primes > 3 are of the form 6k ± 1. Test only those candidates.
Example: Is 149 prime? √149 ≈ 12.2. Test 7, 11 (the 6k±1 values ≤ 12): neither divides 149 → Prime.

Primes up to 30 — Blue = Prime · Gray = Composite

Factor Count Formula

Number of Divisors

If N = p₁ᵃ × p₂ᵇ × p₃ᶜ × … (prime factorisation), then:
Total factors = (a+1)(b+1)(c+1)…
Odd factors: set the power of 2 to 0, apply formula.
Sum of factors = (1+p₁+p₁²+…+p₁ᵃ)(1+p₂+…+p₂ᵇ)… [product of GP sums]
Product of all factors = N^(τ(N)/2), where τ(N) = total number of factors.

Q: Find total factors, odd factors, and even factors of 720.

720 = 2⁴ × 3² × 5¹ Total factors = (4+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 5 × 3 × 2 = 30 Odd factors → force power of 2 to 0: (0+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 1 × 3 × 2 = 6 (The 6 odd factors are: 1, 3, 5, 9, 15, 45) Even factors = Total − Odd = 30 − 6 = 24

Euler's Totient φ(n)

φ(n) — Count of Integers Co-prime to n

φ(n) = count of integers from 1 to n where GCD(k, n) = 1.
Formula: φ(n) = n × ∏(1 − 1/p) for each prime p | n.
φ(12) = 12 × (1−½) × (1−⅓) = 4  →  co-primes are {1, 5, 7, 11} ✓
φ(prime p) = p−1  |  φ(pⁿ) = pⁿ − pⁿ⁻¹  |  φ(mn) = φ(m)φ(n) when GCD(m,n)=1

Fermat's Little Theorem: If p is prime, GCD(a,p)=1 → aᵖ⁻¹ ≡ 1 (mod p).
Euler's Theorem (generalised): a^φ(n) ≡ 1 (mod n) when GCD(a,n)=1.

Find 3¹⁰⁰ mod 7.

φ(7) = 6 (since 7 is prime) By Euler: 3⁶ ≡ 1 (mod 7) 100 = 16 × 6 + 4 → 3¹⁰⁰ = (3⁶)¹⁶ × 3⁴ ≡ 1¹⁶ × 81 ≡ 81 mod 7 81 = 11×7 + 4 → Answer: 3¹⁰⁰ ≡ 4 (mod 7)

Modular Arithmetic & Last-Digit Cycles

Last Digit of Powers

2, 3, 7, 8: cycle of 4 last digits
4, 9: cycle of 2 (4→6→4; 9→1→9)
0, 1, 5, 6: always same last digit
Find last digit: compute exponent mod 4, look up cycle position.

Last Two Digits

Last two digits = number mod 100.
φ(100) = 40, so for GCD(a,100)=1: a⁴⁰ ≡ 1 (mod 100).
Find n mod 40, compute aⁿ mod 100.
Example: 7⁸⁶ → 86 mod 40 = 6 → 7⁶ = 117649 → last two: 49.

Chinese Remainder Theorem

Solve: x ≡ r₁ (mod n₁) and x ≡ r₂ (mod n₂), GCD(n₁,n₂)=1.
Method: List multiples of n₁ giving r₁. Find one that gives r₂ mod n₂.
Ex: x≡3(mod 5), x≡2(mod 7). List: 3,8,13,18,23. 23 mod 7=2 ✓.

B.1   Core Counting Framework GATE Tested

Permutation — Order Matters

Choose and arrange r items from n distinct items.
nPr = n! / (n−r)!
Example: Top-3 rank from 10 students = 10P3 = 10×9×8 = 720.

Combination — Order Ignored

Choose r items from n distinct items (groups, not arrangements).
nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]
Example: Committee of 3 from 10 = 10C3 = 120.

"Must stay together" — Bundle Trick: Treat the group as one unit. If 3 people must always sit together in a row of 8: treat them as 1 unit → 6 units total → 6! arrangements × 3! internal arrangements = 4320.

"Must NOT be together" — Always use complement: Not-together = Total − Always-together. Trying to count "not together" directly is 3× harder. Always subtract from total.

In how many ways can 6 men and 4 women be seated in a row so that no two women are adjacent?

Step 1: Arrange 6 men: 6! = 720 ways Step 2: Identify gaps — between and around 6 men there are 7 gaps: _ M _ M _ M _ M _ M _ M _ Step 3: Place 4 women in 7 gaps (order matters): 7P4 = 7×6×5×4 = 840 Answer: 720 × 840 = 604,800

B.2   Binomial Theorem & Key Identities Interview Level

Binomial Expansion

(x + y)ⁿ = Σ nCk · xⁿ⁻ᵏ · yᵏ for k = 0 to n
General term: T_{k+1} = nCk · xⁿ⁻ᵏ · yᵏ
Sum of all binomial coefficients = 2ⁿ (set x=y=1)
Middle term (n even): T_{n/2+1} = nC(n/2) · x^(n/2) · y^(n/2)
To find term independent of x: set the exponent of x = 0, solve for k.

Find the term independent of x in (x + 1/x²)⁹.

General term = 9Ck · x^(9−k) · (x^−2)^k = 9Ck · x^(9−k−2k) = 9Ck · x^(9−3k) Set exponent = 0: 9 − 3k = 0 → k = 3 Term = 9C3 = 84

Pascal's Rule & Key Identities

Pascal's Rule

nCr + nC(r−1) = (n+1)Cr
Each entry in Pascal's triangle is the sum of the two entries above it.

Symmetry

nCr = nC(n−r)
So nC2 = nC(n−2), etc. Quickly simplify when r is large.

Vandermonde's Identity

Σ (rCk)(n−rC(m−k)) = nCm
Combining selections from two separate groups.

Alternating Sum

nC0 − nC1 + nC2 − … = 0 for n≥1
Set x=−1, y=1 in binomial theorem.

C.1   AP and GP — All Formulas GATE Tested

AP (Linear) vs GP (Exponential) — Growth Comparison
Arithmetic Progression (AP)

nth term: aₙ = a + (n−1)d
Sum of n terms: Sₙ = n/2 · (2a + (n−1)d)
Also: Sₙ = n/2 · (a + last term)
AM: (a + l) / 2
3-term AP setup: Use (a−d), a, (a+d) → sum = 3a, solving is clean.

Geometric Progression (GP)

nth term: aₙ = a · rⁿ⁻¹
Sum of n terms: Sₙ = a(rⁿ−1)/(r−1), r≠1
Infinite sum: S∞ = a/(1−r), |r|<1
GM: √(first × last)
3-term GP setup: Use a/r, a, ar → product = a³.

C.2   HP, AGP and AM≥GM≥HM Inequality Interview Level

Harmonic Progression (HP)

A sequence is in HP if its reciprocals form an AP. There is no direct sum formula — always convert to AP, solve, then convert back.

Harmonic Mean

For two numbers a and b: HM = 2ab/(a+b)
For three terms a, b, c in HP: b = 2ac/(a+c)
HM is always used for average speed over equal distances.

AM ≥ GM ≥ HM — The Golden Inequality

AM ≥ GM ≥ HM for any two positive numbers a, b

Arithmetico-Geometric Progression (AGP)

AGP — Each term = (AP term) × (GP term)

Form: a, (a+d)r, (a+2d)r², …
Infinite AGP sum (|r|<1): S = a/(1−r) + dr/(1−r)²
Finite AGP: Multiply S by r → get rS. Subtract to get S−rS, which telescopes.

Find S = 1 + 2(½) + 3(¼) + 4(⅛) + …

S = 1 + 2(1/2) + 3(1/4) + 4(1/8) + … S/2 = 1(1/2) + 2(1/4) + 3(1/8) + … [multiply by r = ½] ────────────────────────────────────────── S − S/2 = 1 + (1/2) + (1/4) + (1/8) + … [subtract: the "shifts" cancel] S/2 = 1/(1 − ½) = 2 ∴ S = 4

log_b(x) = y means bʸ = x.

log₂(8) = 3 because 2³ = 8  |  log₁₀(1000) = 3 because 10³ = 1000.

⚠ Common Mistakes

log(x+y) ≠ log(x) + log(y) — The product rule applies to log(xy), not log(x+y).
Domain: log_b(x) requires x > 0 and b > 0, b ≠ 1. Always check domain when solving.
Sign reversal: If 0 < b < 1, log_b is a decreasing function — inequality direction reverses!

Solve: log₂(x) + log₂(x−2) = 3

log₂(x(x−2)) = 3 [product rule] x(x−2) = 2³ = 8 [convert to exponential form] x² − 2x − 8 = 0 (x−4)(x+2) = 0 → x = 4 or x = −2 Domain check: x > 0 AND x−2 > 0 → x > 2 x = 4 ✓ (satisfies x > 2) x = −2 ✗ (negative — outside domain) Answer: x = 4

If log₁₀(2) = 0.3010, find the number of digits in 2⁵⁰.

Number of digits in N = ⌊log₁₀(N)⌋ + 1 log₁₀(2⁵⁰) = 50 × log₁₀(2) = 50 × 0.3010 = 15.05 Number of digits = ⌊15.05⌋ + 1 = 15 + 1 = 16
CSE Connection — Why Logs Appear in Algorithms

Binary Search O(log₂ n): Each step halves the problem. After k steps: n/2ᵏ=1 → k=log₂n comparisons. For n=1000: log₂(1000) ≈ 10 comparisons.
Merge Sort O(n log n): n log n is the theoretical minimum for comparison-based sorting (from information theory: log₂(n!) ≈ n log₂ n by Stirling's approximation).

Modulus (Absolute Value)

|x| = x if x≥0, −x if x<0. Geometrically: distance from 0.
|x| = k → x = ±k (two solutions, k≥0)
|x| < k → −k < x < k (open interval, k>0)
|x| > k → x < −k OR x > k (two rays)
Triangle inequality: |a+b| ≤ |a|+|b| and |a−b| ≥ ||a|−|b||

E.1   Base Systems — Concepts & Conversions GATE + Digital Logic

What a Base Means

In base B, each digit position represents a power of B. The digit must be 0 to B−1.
(1011)₂ = 1×2³ + 0×2² + 1×2¹ + 1×2⁰ = 8+0+2+1 = 11 in decimal.
Common bases: Binary (2), Octal (8), Decimal (10), Hexadecimal (16).
Hex digits: 10=A, 11=B, 12=C, 13=D, 14=E, 15=F.

Decimal → Base B (Repeated Division)

Convert 156 to binary:

156 ÷ 2 = 78 rem 0 ← 78 ÷ 2 = 39 rem 0 ← 39 ÷ 2 = 19 rem 1 ← 19 ÷ 2 = 9 rem 1 ← 9 ÷ 2 = 4 rem 1 ← 4 ÷ 2 = 2 rem 0 ← 2 ÷ 2 = 1 rem 0 ← 1 ÷ 2 = 0 rem 1 ← (top) Read BOTTOM to TOP: 10011100₂ Verify: 128+16+8+4 = 156 ✓

Base B → Decimal (Positional Expansion)

Convert (3A7)₁₆ to decimal:

3×16² + A×16¹ + 7×16⁰ = 3×256 + 10×16 + 7×1 = 768 + 160 + 7 = 935

Binary ↔ Hex shortcut (no decimal needed): Group binary digits in sets of 4 from right. Each group maps to one hex digit. 10011100₂ → 1001 1100 → 9 C → 9C₁₆. Works because 2⁴=16.

Binary ↔ Octal: Group in sets of 3 from right (since 2³=8). Much faster than going through decimal.

Arithmetic in Non-Decimal Bases

Key Rule

Carry happens when a digit sum reaches B (not 10). Borrow from next column gives B units (not 10). Work column by column exactly like base-10 addition/subtraction, just with a different carry threshold.

G.2   Time and Work Core

Core Principle — Work as Rate

If A completes a job in a days, A's rate = 1/a of the job per day.
Combined rate = sum of individual rates → combined time = ab/(a+b) for two people.
For three: time = 1/(1/a + 1/b + 1/c).

H.1   Percentages — All Patterns GATE Tested

Successive % Changes

Multiply multipliers, don't add: +30% then −20% = 1.3 × 0.8 = 1.04 = +4% net, not +10%.
Two equal and opposite ±x% → net change = −x²/100 % (always a loss).

% Change Formula

(New − Old) / Old × 100
If a price rises by x%, to get original: divide by (1+x/100), not subtract x%.

Population Problems

After n years at R%: P × (1+R/100)ⁿ
Same as compound interest — identical formula, different context.

Alligation Rule: To mix two items at prices c₁ and c₂ to get a mixture at price m:
Ratio of quantities = (c₂ − m) : (m − c₁). Draw a cross: put m in center, c₁ and c₂ on sides; diagonals give the ratio.

Ratio & Proportion

Componendo-Dividendo

If a/b = c/d, then (a+b)/(a−b) = (c+d)/(c−d). Powerful shortcut to avoid messy substitutions in ratio problems.

Inverse Proportion

More workers → fewer days → inversely proportional. Always write: if x₁y₁ = x₂y₂ (when inversely proportional). Don't set up ratios blindly — think about the relationship first.

Mixture Problems

After removing x litres of mixture and adding x litres of pure liquid repeatedly, fraction of original liquid left = ((V−x)/V)ⁿ, where V = vessel volume, n = number of times.

I.2   Calendar Problems — Odd Days Method GATE Tested

Odd Days

Odd days = total days mod 7. Encoding: 0=Sun, 1=Mon, 2=Tue, 3=Wed, 4=Thu, 5=Fri, 6=Sat.
Ordinary year: 365 = 52×7 + 1 → 1 odd day.
Leap year: 366 = 52×7 + 2 → 2 odd days.
100 years: 76 ordinary (76) + 24 leap (48) = 124 ≡ 5 odd days.
400 years: Gregorian cycle repeats perfectly → 0 odd days.

Leap Year Rules — Check in Order!

1. Divisible by 4 → Leap year.
2. But divisible by 100 → NOT a leap year (overrides rule 1).
3. But divisible by 400 → IS a leap year (overrides rule 2).
∴ 1900 is NOT leap (÷100, not ÷400). 2000 IS leap (÷400). 2100 is NOT leap.

J.2   The 28-Trap Watchlist — Every Exam Trick Must Read

These are the exact errors that distinguish 90-percentile from 70-percentile performance. Study them as a list — each one has cost candidates marks in real exams.

1
Wrong

Average speed = arithmetic mean of speeds

Correct

Average speed = 2ab/(a+b) (Harmonic Mean) for equal distances. AM is only correct for equal times.

2
Wrong

Successive % changes: 30% up then 20% down = +10% net

Correct

Multiply multipliers: 1.3 × 0.8 = 1.04 = +4% net. Always multiply, never add.

3
Wrong

Profit% is sometimes calculated on selling price

Correct

Profit% is ALWAYS on Cost Price by default. If problem specifies "on SP", convert first.

4
Wrong

Circular arrangement = n! (same as linear)

Correct

(n−1)! — one position is fixed to eliminate rotational equivalence.

5
Wrong

CI and SI are different from year 1

Correct

CI = SI for exactly 1 year. They diverge from year 2. CI−SI(2yr) = P(R/100)².

6
Wrong

Infinite GP sum works for any ratio r

Correct

Only valid for |r| < 1. For |r| ≥ 1, the series diverges — no finite sum exists.

7
Wrong

log(x+y) = log x + log y

Correct

log(xy) = log x + log y. Product rule applies to multiplication, NOT addition inside the log.

8
Wrong

f(x−c) shifts the graph LEFT by c

Correct

f(x−c) shifts RIGHT by c. To get f(0), you now need x=c. Counterintuitive but correct.

9
Wrong

Div by 11: use regular digit sum (÷11 → ÷3 rule by mistake)

Correct

ALTERNATING digit sum: (odd positions) − (even positions) = 0 or ÷11. Regular sum tests ÷3, ÷9.

10
Wrong

(a+b)² = a² + b²

Correct

(a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b². The 2ab cross-term is always missing in wrong answers.

11
Wrong

Leap year: divisible by 4 → always leap

Correct

÷100 overrides (not leap), unless ÷400 (then IS leap). 1900: not leap. 2000: leap. 2100: not leap.

12
Wrong

Slant height of cone = vertical height h

Correct

Slant l = √(r²+h²). Use l in lateral SA formula πrl. Never substitute h for l.

13
Wrong

Area of rhombus = side² (same as square)

Correct

Rhombus area = d₁d₂/2 (half-product of diagonals). You cannot find area from side alone.

14
Wrong

Similar triangle area ratio = side ratio

Correct

Area ratio = SQUARE of side ratio. Sides 2:3 → areas 4:9. Volume ratio = CUBE of side ratio.

15
Wrong

HM = (a+b)/2

Correct

(a+b)/2 is the AM. HM = 2ab/(a+b). For speed over equal distances, always use HM.

16
Wrong

Read base-conversion remainders top-to-bottom

Correct

Read remainders BOTTOM to TOP. The last remainder is the most significant digit.

17
Wrong

log domain has no restriction

Correct

log_b(x) requires x>0 AND b>0, b≠1. Always verify domain before accepting solutions.

18
Wrong

|x| = k has one solution

Correct

|x| = k has TWO solutions: x=k and x=−k (for k≥0). |x| = −k has NO solution.

19
Wrong

Binary subtraction: borrowing gives 10 (like decimal)

Correct

Binary borrow gives 2 (the base). Octal borrow gives 8. Borrow = the base value, not 10.

20
Wrong

Multiplying inequality by a variable is always safe

Correct

Multiplying by a negative REVERSES the sign. Never multiply by a variable without knowing its sign.

21
Wrong

Volume conserved in melting → shape stays the same

Correct

Volume is conserved, shape changes. Set V₁ = V₂ and solve for the unknown dimension of the new shape.

22
Wrong

0⁰ = 1 (always)

Correct

0⁰ is indeterminate. In some contexts (combinatorics) it's defined as 1 by convention, but mathematically it's indeterminate.

23
Wrong

Discount and profit% both operate on the same base

Correct

Discount is on Marked Price. Profit% is on Cost Price. Different bases — don't confuse them.

24
Wrong

LCM × HCF = product of numbers (for 3 numbers too)

Correct

LCM × HCF = a × b holds ONLY for exactly two numbers. For three or more, this relationship does not hold.

25
Wrong

GP ratio r can be anything for infinite sum to exist

Correct

Infinite GP sum S∞ = a/(1−r) is ONLY valid when |r| < 1. For |r| ≥ 1, the series diverges.

26
Wrong

Euler's theorem: always a^(p−1) ≡ 1 (mod p)

Correct

Fermat's little theorem uses (p−1) only when p is prime. Euler's general theorem uses φ(n) for any n.

27
Wrong

Trains: crossing time = train length / speed

Correct

When crossing a platform: time = (train + platform length) / speed. Only for a pole/person: time = train length / speed.

28
Wrong

The ÷11 rule uses regular sum of digits

Correct

÷11 uses ALTERNATING digit sum. Regular digit sum is for ÷3 and ÷9. This confusion is the most common divisibility error.
